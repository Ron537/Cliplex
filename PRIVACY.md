# Cliplex Privacy

Cliplex is designed to be **private by default**. Your clipboard is some of the
most sensitive data on your machine, and Cliplex treats it that way.

## No telemetry, no network

- Cliplex makes **no network requests**. It has no analytics, no crash
  reporting, no "phone home", and no auto-update telemetry.
- There is no account, no cloud, and no sync. Everything stays on your device.
- The app's Content-Security-Policy disallows remote content; the UI loads only
  local assets.

## What is stored, and where

- Clipboard history and snippets are stored in a **local SQLite database** at
  your platform's application-data directory (e.g. on macOS:
  `~/Library/Application Support/com.rborysowski.cliplex/cliplex.db`).
- The database is **not encrypted at rest** in this release. Optional encryption
  (SQLCipher) is planned. Until then, rely on your operating system's full-disk
  encryption (FileVault / BitLocker / LUKS).

## What is automatically excluded

- **Concealed / secret clips are skipped.** When an app marks copied content as
  secret using the platform conventions, Cliplex does not store it:
  - macOS: `org.nspasteboard.ConcealedType`, `…TransientType`,
    `…AutoGeneratedType` (the nspasteboard.org convention used by password
    managers).
- **A default app-exclusion list** covers common password managers (1Password,
  Bitwarden, LastPass, KeePassXC, Enpass, macOS Keychain). Clips copied while one
  of these apps is frontmost are never stored. You can edit this list.
- Empty clips are ignored.

## Your control

- Delete any entry, clear history, or clear all (optionally keeping pinned
  items).
- Configure the excluded-app list, history size, and whether concealed clips are
  ignored.
- Pruning automatically removes the oldest unpinned entries beyond your
  configured maximum.

## Permissions

- **Accessibility / input** permission is required to paste into other apps
  (Cliplex synthesizes the paste shortcut). It is used only for that.
